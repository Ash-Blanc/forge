This is Joon Sung Park's PhD dissertation from Stanford University titled "Generative Agent Simulations of Human Behavior," submitted in August 2025. The dissertation explores using large language models to create believable computational agents that can simulate human behavior in interactive environments.

The dissertation presents a comprehensive framework for building what Park calls "generative agents" - computational agents powered by LLMs that can maintain coherent behavior over time through three key capabilities:

1. **Memory** - A structured system for storing and retrieving experiences
2. **Reflection** - Periodically synthesizing higher-level insights from memories
3. **Planning** - Creating and revising multi-timescale action plans

The work demonstrates these agents in "Smallville," a sandbox environment similar to The Sims, where 25 agents interact, form relationships, spread information, and coordinate activities like planning a Valentine's Day party.

The research makes several key contributions:
- An architecture for creating believable, interactive agents with long-term coherence
- Methods for constructing agents from real individual data
- A measurement framework for evaluating simulation accuracy
- A large-scale simulation of 1,000 agents representing U.S. adults
- Applications to platform design and policy through "social simulacra"

The dissertation addresses both the technical challenges of creating coherent AI agents and the ethical considerations around using such simulations, emphasizing the need for transparency, validation, and responsible deployment.

Based on the dissertation, here are practical applications and use cases beyond pure simulation:

## **Product & Platform Design**

**Problem it solves:** Avoiding costly failures and user harm from untested features

- **Social media feature testing**: Before launching a new commenting system, recommendation algorithm, or community feature, populate it with diverse agent populations to identify how trolling, echo chambers, or unintended behaviors might emerge
- **Platform governance**: Test different moderation policies, community guidelines, or intervention strategies to see which actually reduce harm
- **Cold start problem**: New social platforms struggle to attract users when empty—agents can bootstrap initial activity to demonstrate value

## **Training & Professional Development**

**Problem it solves:** Expensive, risky, or ethically difficult training scenarios

- **Difficult conversations**: Managers practicing firing employees, doctors delivering bad news, teachers handling classroom conflicts—without traumatizing real people during practice
- **Crisis response**: Emergency responders or crisis hotline operators practicing with agents representing people in various mental states
- **Sales and negotiation**: Practicing with agents that have different personalities, cultural backgrounds, and negotiation styles
- **Bias training**: Creating scenarios where implicit biases can be safely surfaced and examined

## **Healthcare Applications**

**Problem it solves:** Testing interventions without risking patient harm

- **Clinical trial design**: Simulating patient populations to optimize trial protocols, identify potential dropouts, or predict adherence patterns before recruiting real patients
- **Health communication**: Testing how different patient demographics might respond to medication instructions, health warnings, or treatment options
- **Therapy practice**: Training therapists with agents representing diverse mental health presentations and cultural backgrounds
- **Hospital workflow**: Testing new patient intake processes or care coordination systems

## **Policy & Public Services**

**Problem it solves:** Understanding policy impacts before implementation

- **Public health messaging**: Testing COVID-19 communication strategies across different communities before rollout
- **Benefits programs**: Understanding how different populations navigate social services, identifying barriers
- **Urban planning**: How might communities respond to new zoning laws, transit options, or public spaces?
- **Emergency communications**: Testing evacuation messaging or disaster response protocols

## **Market Research & Consumer Insights**

**Problem it solves:** Expensive and slow traditional research methods

- **Concept testing**: Rapid iteration on product ideas, packaging, messaging with diverse consumer agents
- **Customer journey mapping**: Understanding how different customer types navigate complex purchasing decisions
- **Pricing strategy**: Testing price sensitivity across market segments
- **Brand positioning**: Exploring how messaging resonates with different audiences

## **Education & Learning Systems**

**Problem it solves:** One-size-fits-all education

- **Adaptive tutoring**: Agents that learn individual student approaches to create personalized learning paths
- **Classroom simulation**: Teachers testing different pedagogical approaches with simulated student populations
- **Peer learning**: Creating study partners or discussion participants for isolated learners
- **Historical scenarios**: Students interacting with agents representing historical figures or cultural contexts

## **Content Creation & Entertainment**

**Problem it solves:** Static, scripted experiences

- **Interactive storytelling**: NPCs in games that truly remember and respond to player actions
- **Personalized narratives**: Stories that adapt to individual reader preferences and emotional responses
- **Worldbuilding**: Authors testing whether their fictional societies would actually function as intended
- **Player matching**: Understanding player types to improve multiplayer matchmaking

## **Organizational Design**

**Problem it solves:** Costly organizational restructuring failures

- **Team dynamics**: Testing how different team compositions might collaborate or conflict
- **Communication flows**: Understanding how information spreads through different org structures
- **Change management**: Predicting resistance to organizational changes across different employee segments
- **Remote work policies**: Testing hybrid work arrangements before implementation

## **Legal & Ethical Review**

**Problem it solves:** Limited ability to anticipate edge cases

- **Contract interpretation**: Simulating how different parties might interpret ambiguous clauses
- **Ethics committee scenarios**: Exploring ethical dilemmas before encountering them in practice
- **Regulatory compliance**: Testing whether new rules might have unintended consequences

## **Accessibility & Inclusive Design**

**Problem it solves:** Designing for populations designers don't represent

- **Testing with diverse abilities**: Simulating users with different cognitive, physical, or sensory capabilities
- **Cultural localization**: Understanding how products/messages translate across cultures
- **Age-inclusive design**: Testing interfaces with agents representing different age groups and tech literacy

## **Key Advantages Over Alternatives**

1. **Speed**: Iterate in hours/days vs. weeks/months of user research
2. **Safety**: Test harmful scenarios without exposing real people
3. **Scale**: Evaluate with thousands of diverse "participants" 
4. **Counterfactuals**: Explore "what-if" scenarios impossible in real world
5. **Ethics**: Study sensitive topics without privacy violations or deception
6. **Cost**: Dramatically cheaper than large-scale human studies

## **Critical Limitations to Consider**

- **Not a replacement for human research**—use for hypothesis generation and early-stage exploration
- **Bias inheritance**: Agents reflect biases in training data
- **Validation required**: Must verify against real behavior for high-stakes decisions
- **Known unknowns**: Can't capture what wasn't in training data
- **Context limits**: Most accurate for scenarios similar to training data

The dissertation emphasizes using these as **instruments for exploration** rather than oracles—they help ask better questions and avoid obvious failures, but shouldn't substitute for engaging with real people in consequential domains.

# **Software Engineering Applications of Generative Agents**

## **Development Process & Team Dynamics**

**Problem: Understanding how development practices affect real teams**

- **Agile process testing**: Simulate how different team compositions (junior vs senior ratios, personality types, work styles) respond to sprint lengths, stand-up formats, or retrospective structures before imposing them on real teams
- **Code review culture**: Test different review guidelines, response time expectations, or critique styles to see which promote learning vs. cause friction
- **On-call rotation impact**: Simulate burnout patterns, knowledge distribution, and response quality across different on-call schedules
- **Remote vs. hybrid policies**: Model how different work arrangements affect collaboration, knowledge sharing, and code quality

## **Requirements & Design Validation**

**Problem: Miscommunication between stakeholders causes costly rework**

- **Stakeholder simulation**: Populate requirements meetings with agents representing different user types, product managers, and engineers to surface conflicting needs early
- **User story validation**: Test whether user stories are actually understandable and implementable by simulating developers with different experience levels attempting to build them
- **API design testing**: Before finalizing API contracts, simulate diverse consumer teams trying to integrate—identify confusing patterns or missing functionality
- **Feature flag rollout**: Model how different user segments adopt new features, find bugs, or experience degraded performance

## **Testing & Quality Assurance**

**Problem: Can't test every user scenario; edge cases emerge in production**

- **Behavioral test generation**: Agents with different goals and contexts interact with software to generate realistic test scenarios that QA wouldn't think of
- **Exploratory testing**: Agents "use" the software like real users with specific jobs-to-be-done, uncovering usability issues and unexpected interaction patterns
- **Load testing with realistic behavior**: Instead of uniform request patterns, generate traffic that mimics real user journeys—browsing, searching, purchasing with realistic timing
- **Accessibility testing**: Agents simulating users with different abilities, assistive technologies, or cognitive approaches

## **Developer Tools & IDE Enhancement**

**Problem: Tools don't adapt to individual developer context and workflow**

- **Contextual code completion**: Instead of just syntactic suggestions, agents that understand your current task, codebase conventions, and past decisions
- **Pair programming assistant**: An agent that remembers your project goals, architectural decisions, and coding style to provide relevant suggestions
- **Debugging partner**: Agent that asks clarifying questions, suggests hypotheses, and recalls similar bugs you've encountered
- **Refactoring advisor**: Suggests refactorings based on understanding of your codebase evolution and team's tolerance for change

## **Code Review & Knowledge Transfer**

**Problem: Reviews are inconsistent; knowledge silos form**

- **Review training**: Junior developers practice giving/receiving code reviews with agents that simulate different seniority levels and communication styles
- **Automated reviewer personas**: Agents that provide different perspectives—security-focused, performance-focused, readability-focused—to catch issues human reviewers miss
- **Knowledge capture**: Agents that observe code review discussions and synthesize institutional knowledge (why we chose this pattern, what migrations we've done)
- **Onboarding simulation**: New hires interact with agents representing team members to learn codebase, norms, and decision-making processes

## **Project Management & Planning**

**Problem: Estimates are wrong; dependencies overlooked**

- **Sprint planning simulation**: Agents representing team members with actual velocity data, working styles, and dependencies attempt to complete the proposed sprint—surface over-commitments
- **Risk identification**: Simulate project execution with agents that have realistic blocker patterns (sick days, dependency delays, scope creep) to identify risks
- **Technical debt modeling**: Understand how different debt paydown strategies affect team velocity and morale over time
- **Hiring impact**: Model how adding engineers with different skill sets affects delivery timelines (accounting for ramp-up, communication overhead)

## **Bug Triage & Incident Response**

**Problem: Too many bugs; unclear prioritization**

- **User impact simulation**: Agents representing different user types encounter bugs to assess real-world severity vs. theoretical severity
- **Incident response practice**: Engineers practice incident response with simulated outages, stakeholder pressure, and information uncertainty
- **Runbook validation**: Test incident runbooks with agents following instructions to find gaps, ambiguities, or outdated steps

## **Documentation & Developer Experience**

- **Tutorial validation**: New developer agents follow getting-started guides to ensure they actually work and make sense
- **API documentation quality**: Simulate developers with different use cases trying to understand and use your API from docs alone
- **Error message improvement**: Test whether error messages actually help agents (and thus users) diagnose and fix problems

- **Technology evaluation**: Simulate teams adopting new frameworks/languages to understand learning curve and productivity impacts

## **Security & Compliance**

## **Key Insight for SWE**

Software engineering is fundamentally a **human coordination problem** disguised as a technical one. Most bugs, delays, and failures stem from miscommunication, misunderstanding, and misaligned incentives—not lack of technical skill.

Generative agents let you:
- **Test the meta-process** (how you build) not just the product
- **Identify social/organizational bugs** before they cause technical bugs  
- **Optimize for human experience** of developers, not just code quality
- **Practice difficult scenarios** without team morale costs

The dissertation's framework of **memory, reflection, and planning** maps naturally to software development: engineers remember past decisions, reflect on patterns, and plan implementations. Agents can simulate this at scale to improve both tools and processes.
